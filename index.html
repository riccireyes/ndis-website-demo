Who We Are

Our Heart &
Approach

At Prime Clinical Nursing, we believe that great healthcare is about so much more than just medical support. To us, it's about the freedom to wake up in your own home, stay active in your community, and live a life that you like. We see clinical support as the quiet, steady foundation that helps you stay independent and connected to the people and places you love.

Getting Started

How It Works

Getting started with Prime Clinical Nursing is straightforward. We guide you every step of the way.

01

Get in Touch

Reach out via phone or email. We'll have a friendly conversation about your needs and goals to determine how we can best support you.

02

Assessment & Planning

Our Clinical Manager or one of the directors will conduct face-to-face assessments with you, your family and/or advocate. Together, we develop your person-centred Support Plan.

03

Matched Support Workers

We carefully match you with workers who have the right skills, considering your personality, interests, cultural background, and specific support needs. You'll have a meet-and-greet before services start.

04

Ongoing Review & Support

We regularly review your Support Plan to check that your needs are being met. Reviews happen at least annually, and your Service Agreement can be renegotiated as your goals evolve.

Your Rights

We Protect & Uphold Your Rights

As an NDIS participant, you have many rights. We support and assist you in identifying and exercising these rights to achieve your goals.

Safety & Protection

You have the right to receive support that is free from violence, abuse, neglect, exploitation, or discrimination. We follow the National Principles for Child Safe Organisations.

Freedom & Self-Determination

You have the right to freedom of expression, self-determination, and personal decision-making. We always assume you can make your own choices unless there is clear evidence otherwise.

Privacy & Dignity

We respect your right to privacy and the confidentiality of your personal information. We comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and all Australian Privacy Principles.

Independence & Choice

You have the right to exercise informed choice and control, to be helped to make choices that maximise your independence, and to access supports that respect your culture, diversity, values and beliefs.

Competent Workers

You have the right to receive services from workers who are competent, appropriately qualified, and have expertise in providing person-centred supports.

Advocacy & Voice

You can ask anyone you trust to be your advocate, or we can help you find a professional advocacy service. Your advocate is welcome at any meeting and can speak on your behalf at any time.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the NDIS and our services.

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is an Australian Government initiative that provides funding and support for people with a permanent and significant disability. It helps participants access the supports they need to live an independent and fulfilling life.

To be eligible for the NDIS, you must have a permanent and significant disability or developmental delay, be an Australian citizen or hold a Permanent Visa or Protected Special Category Visa, be under 65 years of age, and require support from a person or equipment to do everyday activities. To access our services, you must also have an NDIS Plan that identifies services within our registration groups and have available funding. We also provide services on a 'fee for service' basis.

Your Support Plan is person-centred and developed collaboratively with you, your family or advocate, and our team. Our Clinical Manager or one of the directors will conduct face-to-face assessments, record your unique skills, strengths, goals and aspirations, and design a plan that promotes your independence. The Plan is flexible and regularly reviewed to ensure it continues to meet your needs.

Our PRIME values guide every clinical decision and connection we build: P — Person-Centred (you are the expert in your own life), R — Respect & Dignity (honouring your story and home), I — Integrity & Quality (maintaining highest nursing standards), M — Making a Difference (bringing our best nursing skills), and E — Empowerment (your independence is our ultimate goal).

You can also contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission on 1800 035 544, the Commonwealth Ombudsman on 1300 362 072, or NDIS Complaints on 1800 800 110. We will never retaliate against you for making a complaint.
